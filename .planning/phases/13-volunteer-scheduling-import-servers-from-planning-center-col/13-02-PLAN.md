<objective>
Build the phase centerpiece: `proposeQuarterSchedule`, a deterministic, pure, greedy weighted-fair-share scheduler. It processes service dates chronologically, fills each role's slots by picking the candidate furthest below their 1-in-N frequency target, never violates blackout dates or pairings (hard constraints), and reports unfilled slots and pairing conflicts instead of silently bending them.

Purpose: All quarterly scheduling correctness lives here. Isolating it as a pure function (no Firestore, no Date.now) makes every constraint rule unit-testable — the exact pattern already proven by `src/utils/suggestions.ts`.     - blackout: a person with blackoutDates containing date D is never present in calendar[D][any role]; if they were the only candidate the slot appears in `unfilled`
    - multi: a role with count 2 and 3 eligible people gets exactly 2 distinct personIds; a person eligible for two roles on the same date can appear in both roles' arrays (D-04)
    - deficit: given two candidates, the one with higher (dateIndex+1)/frequencyTargetN − servedCount is chosen; a weekly (N=1) never-served person outranks a monthly (N=4) already-served person
    - deficit tie-break: two candidates with identical deficit resolve deterministically (lower servedCount, then name.localeCompare) — running twice yields identical calendars
    - unfilled: a role with zero eligible/available candidates yields a `{date, roleId}` entry in `unfilled` and no fabricated assignment; nobody's servedCount exceeds what their eligibility allows
    - pairing: when person A (paired with B) is chosen on date D, B is also assigned on D in one of B's OWN eligible roles (not necessarily A's role) — D-09
    - pairing conflict: if B is blacked out on D, no assignment for B is made and a `pairingConflicts` entry `{date, personId:A, partnerId:B, reason}` is recorded; B's blackout is NOT violated (D-07 wins)
    - override: resolveRolesForDate returning a per-date override (e.g. count 0 for 'livestream', count 2 for 'vocals') is honored over the default for that date (D-02)
    - consecutive: an N=1 person eligible+available every week IS assigned on consecutive weeks (proves no back-to-back suppression — D-12)
    - fill-gaps: passing existingCalendar with a locked assignment seeds servedCounts so those people's deficit reflects the locked service, and only empty slots get newly filled
